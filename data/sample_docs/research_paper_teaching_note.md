# Research Paper Teaching Note

Title: Working Capital Management and Profitability in Emerging Market Retail Firms

Objective
The paper studies whether working capital management affects profitability in emerging market retail firms. The research problem is important because retailers often hold large inventories and depend on supplier credit.

Research Question
Does a shorter cash conversion cycle improve profitability for retail companies?

Data and Sample
The study uses annual financial statements for 80 listed retail firms from 2018 to 2024. The sample excludes firms with missing inventory, receivables, or payables data.

Variables
The dependent variable is return on assets. The main independent variables are inventory days, receivable days, payable days, and the cash conversion cycle. Control variables include firm size, sales growth, leverage, and inflation.

Methodology
The paper uses panel regression with firm fixed effects. Robust standard errors are applied to reduce the effect of heteroskedasticity.

Main Findings
The study finds that a shorter cash conversion cycle is associated with higher return on assets. Inventory days have the strongest negative relationship with profitability. The authors interpret this as evidence that slow-moving inventory harms retail performance.

Limitations
The paper reports association, not guaranteed causality. The sample focuses on listed retail firms, so results may not generalize to small private companies.
