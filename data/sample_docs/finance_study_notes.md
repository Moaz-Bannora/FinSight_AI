# Finance Study Notes

## Current Ratio

The current ratio measures liquidity. It is calculated as current assets divided by current liabilities. A ratio above 1.0 means current assets exceed current liabilities. A ratio between 1.5 and 3.0 is often considered comfortable, but the best benchmark depends on the industry.

## Debt-to-Equity Ratio

The debt-to-equity ratio measures leverage. It is calculated as total debt divided by total equity. A higher ratio means the company relies more on debt financing. Debt can increase returns during good periods, but it also increases fixed obligations and financial risk.

## Gross Margin

Gross margin is calculated as revenue minus cost of goods sold, divided by revenue, multiplied by 100. It shows how much revenue remains after direct production costs. Declining gross margin may indicate supplier cost pressure, weak pricing power, or operational inefficiency.

## Net Present Value

Net present value discounts expected future cash flows to today's value. A positive NPV suggests that a project creates value under the stated assumptions. NPV depends heavily on forecast quality and the chosen discount rate.

## Return on Equity

Return on equity is net income divided by shareholder equity, multiplied by 100. It measures how effectively a company generates profit from shareholder capital. High ROE should be checked against leverage because debt can amplify ROE.
