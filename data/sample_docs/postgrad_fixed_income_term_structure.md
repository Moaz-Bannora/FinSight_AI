# Fixed Income Pricing and Term Structure

## Bond Price and Yield

Bond price is the present value of coupon and principal payments:

$$P=\sum_{t=1}^{n}\frac{C_t}{(1+y)^t}+\frac{F}{(1+y)^n}$$

Where:

- $C_t$ is coupon payment.
- $F$ is face value.
- $y$ is yield to maturity.

Bond prices and yields move inversely.

## Term Structure of Interest Rates

The term structure describes interest rates across maturities. Common theories include expectations theory, liquidity preference theory, and market segmentation theory.

Forward rates can be derived from spot rates. If $s_1$ is the one-year spot rate and $s_2$ is the two-year spot rate:

$$1+f_{1,1}=\frac{(1+s_2)^2}{1+s_1}$$

Interpretation: the yield curve reflects expectations, risk premia, supply and demand, monetary policy, and liquidity preferences.
