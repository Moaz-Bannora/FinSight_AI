Nile Retail Holdings - Annual Report Excerpt 2025

Business Overview
Nile Retail Holdings operates neighborhood retail stores and an online grocery platform. During 2025, revenue growth improved by 12 percent because of stronger online orders and a larger store network. Management stated that customer retention improved after the loyalty program was redesigned.

Profitability
Gross profit increased, but the gross margin declined from 34 percent to 31 percent because supplier prices and delivery costs increased. Net income remained positive, but management warned that operating expenses grew faster than revenue in the second half of the year.

Liquidity
Current assets were 2,500 million Egyptian pounds and current liabilities were 1,000 million Egyptian pounds at year end. Cash flow from operations remained positive, but inventory growth created working capital pressure. Management said the company would focus on faster inventory turnover and better supplier payment terms.

Solvency and Leverage
Total debt increased from 900 million Egyptian pounds to 1,350 million Egyptian pounds after the company financed new stores with bank loans. Total equity was 1,500 million Egyptian pounds. Interest expense increased, so management identified debt service as a key risk factor for 2026.

Strategic Risks
The main risk factors are inflation, supplier cost pressure, intense competition in online grocery, inventory obsolescence, and higher borrowing costs. Management also noted that technology outages could affect online sales during peak periods.

Management Improvement Plan
The company plans to protect margins through supplier renegotiation, delivery route optimization, private-label products, and better inventory analytics. Management also plans to slow store expansion until debt levels stabilize.
