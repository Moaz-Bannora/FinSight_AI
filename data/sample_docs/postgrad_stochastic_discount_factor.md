# Stochastic Discount Factor

Modern asset pricing can be expressed using a stochastic discount factor $m$:

$$P_t=E_t[m_{t+1}X_{t+1}]$$

For gross returns:

$$1=E_t[m_{t+1}R_{t+1}]$$

The stochastic discount factor framework is powerful because it unifies bond pricing, equity pricing, derivatives, and consumption-based asset pricing.

Interpretation: payoffs are more valuable when they occur in states of the world where marginal utility is high. This makes risk pricing depend on covariance with the discount factor, not just standalone volatility.
