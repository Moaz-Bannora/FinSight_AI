# Credit Spreads, Liquidity Risk, and Stress Testing

## Credit Spreads

Credit spread is the yield difference between a risky bond and a comparable risk-free bond:

$$Credit\ Spread=Yield_{risky}-Yield_{risk\text{-}free}$$

Spreads compensate investors for expected default loss, liquidity risk, risk aversion, and uncertainty.

## Economic Capital

Economic capital is capital held to absorb unexpected losses at a target confidence level. It is often connected to VaR, stress testing, and regulatory capital frameworks.

## Stress Testing

Stress testing evaluates how a portfolio or institution behaves under severe but plausible scenarios.

Examples:

- Interest rates rise sharply.
- Credit spreads widen.
- Revenue falls and working capital needs rise.
- Exchange rates move against the company.
- Liquidity markets freeze.

## Liquidity Risk

Liquidity risk has two meanings:

- Funding liquidity risk: inability to obtain cash or financing.
- Market liquidity risk: inability to sell assets quickly without large price impact.

Interpretation: liquidity risk is important because solvency can deteriorate quickly when funding dries up.
