# CAPM and Multi-Factor Asset Pricing

## Capital Asset Pricing Model

The Capital Asset Pricing Model links expected return to systematic risk:

$$E(R_i)=R_f+\beta_i(E(R_m)-R_f)$$

Where:

- $R_f$ is the risk-free rate.
- $E(R_m)-R_f$ is the market risk premium.
- $\beta_i$ measures sensitivity to market movements.

CAPM is elegant but relies on strong assumptions about markets, investor behavior, borrowing/lending, and diversification.

## Multi-Factor Models

Multi-factor models explain returns using multiple systematic risk factors:

$$R_i-R_f=\alpha_i+\beta_{i,1}F_1+\beta_{i,2}F_2+\cdots+\beta_{i,k}F_k+\epsilon_i$$

Examples include:

- Fama-French three-factor model: market, size, and value.
- Carhart four-factor model: market, size, value, and momentum.
- Fama-French five-factor model: market, size, value, profitability, and investment.

Interpretation: a statistically significant alpha may indicate abnormal performance, omitted risk factors, data mining, or model misspecification.
